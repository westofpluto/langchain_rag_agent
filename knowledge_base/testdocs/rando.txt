The spacecraft hummed quietly as it drifted through the asteroid belt. Captain Rodriguez checked the navigation console for the third time that hour. They were three months into their mission to establish the first mining colony on Europa. The crew of twelve had trained for years, but nothing could truly prepare them for the isolation of deep space. Through the viewport, Jupiter loomed massive and majestic, its swirling storms clearly visible even from this distance. In two weeks, they would begin their descent to the ice-covered moon below, where they hoped to find evidence of life beneath the frozen surface.
